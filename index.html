What We Do

Engineering-Based Cost Segregation Studies

Every study is built on a Detailed Engineering Approach — the IRS’s preferred methodology — combining a professional engineer’s site analysis with CPA-grade tax reporting.

1

New Acquisition Studies

Property purchased in the current tax year. Reclassify 5-, 7-, and 15-year personal property and land improvements to accelerate depreciation in year one.

2

Look-Back Studies

For property placed in service in prior years. We file Form 3115 to claim the cumulative §481(a) catch-up adjustment in the current year — no amended returns required.

3

New Construction

Ground-up builds and major renovations. Direct cost identification from contractor pay applications, change orders, and as-built drawings.

4

Partial Asset Dispositions

Tangible-property regulation (§1.263(a)-3) studies to recognize loss on retired components and avoid recapture surprises.

5

Form 3115 Preparation

Change-in-method filings done right. Includes §481(a) calculation, statements, and dual-copy filing instructions for your tax preparer.

6

IRS Audit Support

Should your study be examined, we stand behind our workpapers and coordinate with your tax representative through the examination cycle.

Our Process

From Site Visit to IRS-Ready Report

Five disciplined steps. Every study aligns with the IRS Cost Segregation Audit Techniques Guide and the 13 quality elements of Publication 5653.

  1. 1

    Engagement & Data

    Engagement letter, conflict check, document request.

  2. 2

    Site Visit

    P.E.-led inspection. Photographic and dimensional evidence.

  3. 3

    Engineering Takeoff

    Quantity survey, RSMeans cost data, indirect-cost allocation.

  4. 4

    Tax Reclassification

    CPA-led MACRS classification, §481(a) calc, Form 3115 if needed.

  5. 5

    Report & Delivery

    Two-principal QC sign-off. Delivery to you and your CPA.
